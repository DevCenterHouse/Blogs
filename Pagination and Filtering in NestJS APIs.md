<p>APIs are the backbone of modern applications. But as your data grows, returning everything in one go becomes inefficient—and even dangerous. That’s where <strong>pagination</strong> and <strong>filtering in NestJS</strong> come in. With these tools, you can control how much data is sent to clients and allow them to query exactly what they need.</p>
<p>In this guide, you’ll learn how to implement <strong><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pagination" rel="noreferrer noopener" target="_blank">pagination</a> and filtering</strong> in your NestJS REST APIs like a pro.</p>
<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Why Pagination and Filtering Matter</h2>
<p>Imagine an e-commerce app listing thousands of products. Without pagination, every API request could pull in all product records, slowing down the frontend and overloading the server.</p>
<p>Filtering, on the other hand, empowers users to narrow down results—like searching for products under a certain price, in a specific category, or created within a date range.</p>
<p>Together, these features create better user experiences and keep your system performant.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Best Practices</h2>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Set max limit</strong>: Avoid abuse by restricting the <code>limit</code> to something like 100 max.</li>
<li><strong>Use DTOs</strong>: For validation, create a DTO with decorators like <code>@IsOptional()</code>, <code>@IsInt()</code>, and use <code>@Query()</code> with <code>ValidationPipe</code>.</li>
<li><strong>Cache frequent queries</strong>: Use in-memory caching (like Redis) for frequently repeated filters.</li>
<li><strong>Use pagination metadata</strong>: Include total count, current page, next/prev flags, etc.</li>
</ul>
